📘 Public Case File – Ongoing Legal Proceedings

RAITANO vs TEMMERMAN

💡 What really happened?

A tenant in Flemish Brabant was targeted by his landlord, despite the absence of a clear procedural framework, concrete evidence, or any proposed solution.

Instead of staying silent, he decided to document everything, archive it, and submit it to the public prosecutor — entirely without a lawyer.

The landlord and his lawyer responded with warnings, vague assertions, and indirect initiatives. But the procedure did not stop.

Today, this case is public. Every step is certified: date, structure, evidence.

This is not a media campaign. It is proof that one citizen — alone — can use the system without resources, but with structure.

🧾 Rental dispute turned criminal case: a two-pronged dynamic

This dispute concerns a rental agreement in Meerbeek (Flemish Brabant) between the company Reinout Temmerman BV (landlord) and the tenants Stefan Raitano and Maria Rizzo.

The initial disagreement related to a retroactive indexation of several hundred euros, which the tenants considered to be in breach of a prior oral agreement. The case was brought before the Justice of the Peace in Leuven (1st canton) and led to the civil judgment of 26 May 2025 (docket 25A782/1).

This judgment acknowledged a partial indexation of €2,173.86, placed the costs on the tenant, dismissed the counterclaim of €57,500, and excluded all exhibits submitted before and after the hearing of 28 April 2025, in accordance with Article 771 of the Belgian Judicial Code. No extrajudicial facts or behaviour were retained.

However, several new facts not examined in the civil proceedings emerged during and after the process:

  • 🔁 Repeated reminders outside the legal framework,
  • 📩 Messages that could be perceived as pressure or injunctions,
  • ⚠️ Conduct deemed intrusive or intimidating.

These elements led to four reports via e-Deposit, before the tenants contacted the public prosecutor directly, without legal counsel or legal protection.

On 19 May 2025, prior to the delivery of the civil judgment, a criminal complaint with constitution of civil party was filed and accepted by the Leuven public prosecutor (ref. COR/470 – PB/2500031), following a thorough review lasting over 1.5 hours.

This complaint targets exclusively criminal offences, unrelated to the civil dispute, without challenging the judgment of 26 May.

Within 33 days, it led to the compilation of a structured body of evidence comprising 49 certified exhibits (PJ1 to PJ49), including:

  • 🔐 verifiable electronic evidence (SHA-256 hashes),
  • 📎 reporting of a unilateral attempt at civil eviction (PJ35),
  • 📤 direct notifications to the prosecutor and justice of the peace.

To date, none of these exhibits has been formally contested, either by the landlord or by their counsel, despite full notification.

This structuring led to the creation of the FX1 website, aimed at documenting a procedural reality ignored in the civil forum, but legally grounded, within a rigorous, loyal, and transparent framework.

The FX1 website was published after the civil ruling, with strict separation of proceedings, in line with case law on freedom of expression and the right to information (GDPR art. 6.1.e/f – ECHR art. 10 – AI Act).
